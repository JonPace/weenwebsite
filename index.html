Ween.com

Sometimes a band comes along, and, well... its the band for its place and time. Ween was formed in New Hope, Pennsylvania in 1984 by childhood friends Aaron Freeman and Mickey Melchiondo, better known by their respective stage names Gene Ween and Dean Ween. While they are generally categorized as an alternative rock band, the band is known for their highly eclectic catalog of songs inspired by funk, soul, country, gospel, prog, psychedelia, R&B, heavy metal, punk rock, and countless more. The band has been widely regarded as irreverent of societal norms and customs. In other words, Ween is one weird band. You've probably never heard of them, but you've undoubtely been exposed to their influence on alternative culture.

GodWeenSatan: The Oneness

The debut album, released on November 16, 1990. The album introduces several key themes for the group, including their eclecticism, gonzo sense of humor, and their demon god/mascot, the Boognish.

The Pod

The second studio album, released on September 20, 1991. Less inflamed and inspired than the first album, The Pod lurches, howls, fuzzes and strums through sloppy creations that were recorded on a Tascam four-track cassette recorder.

Listen
Pure Guava

The third studio album and major label debut, released on November 10, 1992 by Elektra Records. The album features what is perhaps Ween's most well-known song, "Push th' Little Daisies", which gained noteriety after being featured on the MTV show Beavis and Butt-head.

Listen
Chocolate and Cheese

The fourth studio album by the American rock band Ween, originally released on September 27, 1994. It was the first Ween album to be recorded in a professional studio. The song "Roses Are Free" became a favorite of the jam band Phish and has been covered by them live nearly 50 times.

Listen
12 Golden Country Greats

The fifth studio album, released on July 16, 1996. It marked the only time Ween limited themselves to a specific genre of music, in this case, country music. It was recorded at Bradley's Barn in Nashville, Tennesse. Many fans were confused by the radical break from the band's previous sound, and also by the fact that the album only has 10 songs.

Listen
The Mollusk

The sixth studio album, released on June 24, 1997. It is a multi-genre concept album with a dark nautical theme, with most songs incorporating elements from psychedelia and/or sea shanties while also featuring a heavy progressive rock influence. The Mollusk was a direct influence on the animated television series SpongeBob SquarePants.

Listen
White Pepper

The seventh studio album, released on May 2, 2000. White Pepper is one of Ween's most polished albums. The title is said to be a tip of the hat to The Beatles, combining Sgt. Pepper's and The White Album into one name.

Listen
Quebec

The eighth studio album, released on August 5, 2003. A favorite among Ween fans - Quebec represented a return to the "browner side" of the band - highlighting their predlicition to weirdness, both creepy and cheerful, that had largely been absent since Chocolate & Cheese.

Listen
La Cucaracha

The ninth and final studio album, released on October 23, 2007. La Cucaracha was described by the band as being a "party record", as opposed to their prior album Quebec which was more of a Pink Floyd type album.
